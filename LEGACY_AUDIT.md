## 2. Pricing, niche, and unit economics (locked elsewhere, absent here)
Pricing tiers, consistent across the original repo's Constitution and Business Plan: Office Essentials $147/mo (Website Ops only, deliberately agent-free); AI Office Staff (anchor) $497/mo (Website Ops + Service Advisor + 24/7 Receptionist + Lead Coordinator + Reputation Manager); AI Business Manager $997/mo (adds Sales Consultant + Marketing Coordinator + Growth Manager); White-Glove/Multi-Location ~$2,485/mo (adds ServiceTitan/Jobber/Housecall Pro integration). None of this appears anywhere in this repo.

Beachhead niche: HVAC, Phoenix AZ (ICP 3–15 employees, $500K–$3M revenue), then two separate expansion tracks — same-niche (DFW roofing, Tampa plumbing) and a separate geography-first track (water damage/roofing/garage doors across Huntsville AL, Knoxville TN, Greenville SC, Boise ID, Colorado Springs CO, Charleston SC, Madison WI, Des Moines IA) sourced from `city_niche.xlsx` (also on the Desktop) and, per Drive's "Developer tasks" doc, a Grape Leads sourcing plan (8 cities, 5 niches, $340–530 budget).

**CAC / churn / LTV — genuinely inconsistent across every source checked, not just two figures:**
- CAC: $100–200/customer (bootstrap, founder cold outreach — the figure most consistently repeated); $150 (single-point, used in investor talking points); $500 (fully-loaded once founder time is priced); $1,500 ("Financial Model v2," never labeled as a distinct later-stage assumption); $80–150 (paid ads, per Drive's "SAFE_Proposal_Revised"); $20–50 (cold outbound, same doc).
- Churn: 5% monthly ("Financial Model v2," "Business Plan," "Developer tasks" — an unmeasured planning assumption in all three); 8–16% monthly ("Investor's Insights 2.0," claimed "researched," no primary source cited).
- LTV methodology itself differs: a flat 24-month-multiplier method (`Tier 2 = $427 × 24 = $10,248`) is used in the original repo's "Developer tasks" doc, versus a churn-derived method (`GP ÷ monthly churn`) used in the `STARTTTUUUUPPPP` reconciliation, which gives $5,340 at 8% churn or $8,540 at 5% churn for the same tier. These are not the same model and don't cross-check.
- Best-practice reconciliation (from `STARTTTUUUUPPPP/webstaffr_strategy_5_gaps_resolved.md`, itself [Inference], not a rediscovered ground truth): plan at ~$150–500 CAC and 8% monthly churn until 3+ months of real retention data exists.

**Fundraising ask itself also varies:** $10,000 SAFE / $150,000 cap / 6.7% ownership (both the local `docs/investor-proposal.md` and Drive's "investor-proposal.md" and "SAFE_Proposal_Revised" agree on these terms) — but the **use-of-funds breakdown differs by document** (e.g. dev hardware $3,049 new machine vs. $1,500 refurbished Mac Mini), and a separate Drive doc ("Developer tasks") asks for "$15K–$50K SAFE (or RBF)" instead. **Flag before any of this goes to an investor:** the Drive "SAFE_Proposal_Revised" (in the WebStaffr 3.0 Drive folder, so it reads as current) describes a Claude API + Twilio + Voiceflow/Retell/Bland voice stack — not what this repo actually built (Grok/xAI, no Twilio). Sending that document as-is would misdescribe the product to an investor.

## 3. Unmigrated code and assets (original repo)
- **Agent code for 4 of the 9 planned "AI employee" roles**, real and working (not stubs): `backend/agents/{receptionist,lead_coordinator,reputation_manager,website_ops}.py` — Twilio SMS + SendGrid email, calling OpenAI directly (gpt-4o-mini/gpt-4o routing), no CrewAI/LangChain. `agents/workflows.py` defines all 9 roles with system prompts and orchestration (`call_agent()`, `lead_to_booked_workflow()`, `route_event()`). - **11 fully-built demo sites** (`sites/`): hvac, plumber, electrician, roofer, contractor, handyman, landscaper, painter, pest, pool, pressure-wash — each with homepage + 6 service sub-pages. Described in that repo's own status doc as its strongest investor-demo asset.
- **Investor materials:** `docs/investor-proposal.md` (the $10K SAFE) and a full Next.js `investor-site/` (animated Hero/BusinessModel/Competition/Market/AIOffice sections).

Worth a comparison pass if a similar bug is ever suspected in the Lovable-generated site.
- `plugins/marketing-director-gtm/` — already extracted into the `marketing-director-gtm` Cowork skill available in this project; no action needed.
